What it catches

The things that slip through every manual review. Every time.

Human reviewers are excellent at judgment. They are bad at pattern recognition across 500 transactions from 12 clients simultaneously. Aqqrue runs that pattern check continuously and surfaces only the items that fall outside what is normal for that specific client.

Duplicate
AWS Invoice #4421 charged twice — $312.50 x2
Same invoice number, same vendor, same amount, 3 days apart. Likely a double-send from vendor. Flagged before payment.
Unusual Amount
Salesforce charge 340% above monthly average
Client historically pays $290/month. This month shows $986. No new seats added in system. Flagged for client confirmation.
Misclassification Risk
"Stripe payout" categorized as operating expense
This transaction pattern matches revenue inflow, not expense. Auto-categorization overridden and flagged for review.
Vendor Duplicate
"Microsoft 365" and "MSFT Cloud Sub" are the same vendor
Two separate vendor entries in Chart of Accounts for the same underlying subscription. Merge suggested.
Round Number
$5,000 cash withdrawal with no memo
Round-number cash withdrawals with no attached documentation are a common audit flag. Requesting supporting receipt from client.

Most accounting errors are not calculation mistakes. They are duplicate entries, misclassifications, and missing context that accumulate invisibly until an audit makes them visible.

How it learns

Normal is different for every client. Aqqrue knows the difference.

Baseline
Learns what is normal
Aqqrue builds a spending profile for each client. Typical vendors, typical amounts, typical timing, typical categories. Deviations from that profile get a closer look.
Continuous
Runs on every transaction
Every new transaction is checked against the client's historical pattern, against known duplicate signatures, and against accounting rules. Not at month end. The moment it arrives.
Prioritized
Surface-to-review queue
Anomalies are ranked by severity and sent to your review queue with context. You see the flag, the reason, the historical comparison, and a suggested action. One click to resolve.
